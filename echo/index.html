Echo

This collection marks the moment my art ceased to be a private practice and became a necessity. Before the war, concepts lived in the drawer of my mind. But as reality fractured, the urge to materialize these thoughts became overwhelming.

Echo gathers the works that were forced into existence by global tragedy, acting as a language when words are powerless. These pieces document the external collapse of logic and humanity, serving as tangible evidence of a world that has lost its structure.

Not your war

01 / Performance

A durational performance responding to the war through a ritual of breaking and repairing. Using kintsugi, the work focuses on the irreversible and permanent scars left on the collective Self.

Crime or Punishment

02 / Installation

Installation forces a physical shift in perspective to reveal conflicting realities. Juxtaposing state repression with the luxury of its elites, the work centers on a passport image to expose the hypocrisy of global complicity and the vulnerability of individual identity under authoritarianism.

Mordor

03 / Immersive

The immersive installation transforming a room into a living archive of collective memory. By physically peeling back grey sheets to reveal hidden histories, visitors enact the tension between state censorship and the necessity of disclosure.

Puscha

04 / Mixed-Media

A mixed-media landscape where the stitched name of a country visually morphs into “PUSCHA”. This linguistic distortion critiques how the global gaze conflates the country with its regime, mistaking the people for the authoritarian state.

It's just death

05 / Duration

A durational installation positioning the viewer between opposing walls of AI-generated portraits. As faces vanish into black voids or static with a dry mechanical click, the work reduces mass tragedy to a routine data stream, exposing the dehumanizing, statistical nature of modern warfare.

Darling

06 / Installation

A hand-woven carpet crafted from black body-bag plastic, mimicking a field of sedge grass. Through the ritualistic braiding of this synthetic soil, the installation transforms the floor into a texturized mass grave, blurring the boundary between the fallen body and the earth.
