Darling

kamysh #11 Art object

Concept sketch / hand drawing

The chaotic mass of plastic pile is braided into plaits, as an act of mourning and an attempt to manually organize the chaos of death. Braiding becomes the only accessible way to touch the unspeakable, arranging the fallen row by row.

This is a landscape flattened into a mass grave, where the boundary between human and soil is erased.

Darling — for what seemed beautiful. Then for what it covered. The word you use when they have left, when they did not save themselves, when you must still address them.

Object & Process

Specifications

The weaving process is manual; slight variations in braid density and texture are inherent to the recycled material.

Materiality

Hand-woven tapestry. Recycled polyethylene (industrial-grade garbage bags), linen warp and weft. Dimensions: 200 × 150 cm.

Visual Properties

Black-green composite plastic. Appears deep black at rest, reveals dark green undertone when stretched or twisted during knotting and braiding.

Presentation

The pile forms dense vertical strands resembling sedge or reed grass. Lower edge finished with hand-braided tassels. Mounted on rigid metal frame, wall-presented as relief.
