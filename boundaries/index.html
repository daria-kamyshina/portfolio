Boundaries

In Boundaries of Perception, the narrative evolves from the immediate to the existential. I move beyond the social surface to explore the deep, invisible architecture of the human condition. Here, individual trauma intersects with collective blindness (Survival Game), and personal myths (Pink Elephants) become the only way to navigate a fractured reality. These works invite the viewer into a space where the boundary between the external world and the internal self dissolves.
This collection gathers concepts that span different periods of my life. Some originated long before the war, while others emerged in its shadow. What unites them is a shift away from direct documentation toward a more complex, multilayered inquiry.

The Divide

01 / Concept

A minimalist diptych confronting Islamic and European cultural perceptions through opposing canvases. By juxtaposing a black veil of observation against a beige field of blindness, the work uses texture and geometry to materialize the invisible barriers between concealment and exposure.

Glass ceiling

02 / Installation

An installation featuring a low-hanging glass ceiling that forces viewers to stoop, physically enacting the constraints of gender inequality. The breakthroughs in the glass are edged with red crystal beads, symbolizing success while simultaneously evoking the blood and trauma required to pierce the barrier.

Digitalprint

03 / Art Object

An installation envisioning the future fossils of our technogenic era. Inside a black industrial cube, lampwork glass creatures glowing like 3D wireframes float in cold light, presenting a ghostly skeleton of a culture stripped of flesh and reduced to data, glass, and metal.

Survival Game

04 / Installation

A chess installation juxtaposing a massive transparent army of Civilization against a smaller opaque force of Nature. While visually dominant, the human side is strategically hopeless. The work mocks anthropocentric pity, revealing that while we fear for the planet, it is actually humanity that stands in Zugzwang.

Form of existence

05 / Sculpture

A interactive sculpture of a heart pierced by steel rods, which serve as the armature for a new personality. Viewers (or the artist) dress these needles in seven interchangeable skins—from concrete to feathers—ritually transforming the sharp framework of trauma into various defensive architectures.

The Garden

06 / Installation

An optical sanctuary composed of mirrored vases resting on colorful saucers. Through reflection, the vessels absorb the hidden patterns beneath them, manifesting them as shimmering, illusory flowers. The work visualizes the alchemy of creation, where a concealed foundation blooms into visible harmony.

07 / Mirror Tunnel

Between the end and infinity

An infinity mirror tunnel visualizing a child’s solution to an existential paradox. Confronting the terror of a boundless universe, the work populates the void with Pink Elephants, using materialized absurdity to bridge the gap between logical collapse and the incomprehensible.
