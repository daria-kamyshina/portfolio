Between the end
and infinity

kamysh #0 Art object

Concept sketch / hand drawing

Infinity Mirror / Portal effect

My 8-year-old brother, obsessed with physics encyclopedias, tried to explain to me that the universe is infinite. My five-year-old mind collapsed: I couldn't imagine infinity, but I couldn't imagine an end either because something must exist beyond the wall.

Tormented by this logical trap all day, I invented a solution to stop the anxiety: I decided that Pink Elephants live in the gap between the End and Infinity.

This installation is a portal back to that solution. Using an infinity mirror tunnel, I materialized that protective fantasy. It explores how we use absurdity and imagination to reconcile with the terrifying concepts that pure logic cannot resolve.

Object & Process

Specifications

The author reserves the right to vary the number of units and specific pose/drawing of the Pink Elephant, provided the "naive pink character in infinite tunnel" conceptual combination remains central.

Structure & Materiality

Infinity mirror, LED, acrylic markers. Multiple wall-mounted units, each ~50×50×10 cm. Rigid black housing, front semi-transparent mirror, rear fully reflective mirror.

Lighting

UV/LED spectrum energizes fluorescent pigment. The Pink Elephant appears self-luminous, multiplied by mirrors against deep black void.

Content

Hand-drawn Pink Elephant, naive sketch aesthetic, applied directly onto glass with acrylic markers/paint. Each unit unique. The drawing interacts with reflections, endlessly replicating the elephant into the depth of the tunnel.
