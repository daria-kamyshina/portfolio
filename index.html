Portfolio 2024

Tbilisi (Georgia) / Leinfelden (Germany)

My artistic practice is an investigation into the fragility of systems, be they political regimes, psychological defenses, or ecological balances. I work primarily with glass and mixed media because these materials embody a fundamental paradox: they are rigid enough to build structures, yet brittle enough to shatter under sudden impact.

My work oscillates between the personal and the geopolitical. In projects like Not Your War, I explore how individual lives are crushed by the machinery of history. In Form of Existence, I examine the internal architecture of trauma.

My goal is to engage the viewer in a dialogue about the limits of our perception and the resilience of the human spirit in the face of collapse.

Echoes collection

This collection marks the moment my art ceased to be a private practice and became a necessity. Before the war, concepts lived in the drawer of my mind, but as reality fractured, the urge to materialize these thoughts became overwhelming.

Echoes gathers the works that were forced into existence by global tragedy—not your war, mordor, puscha, darling—acting as a language when words are powerless.

Not your war

A durational performance responding to the war in Ukraine through a ritual of breaking and repairing using kintsugi. The work focuses on the irreversible changes and permanent scars left on the collective self.

Crime or Punishment

Installation forces a physical shift in perspective to reveal conflicting realities, juxtaposing Russian state repression with the western luxury of its elites. The work centers on a passport image to expose the hypocrisy of global complicity.

Mordor

An immersive installation transforming a room into a living archive of Russian collective memory. By physically peeling back layers of grey sheets to reveal hidden histories, visitors enact the tension between state censorship and the necessity of disclosure.

Puscha

A mixed media landscape where the stitched word RUSSIA visually morphs into PUSCHA. This linguistic distortion critiques how the global gaze conflates the country with its regime, mistaking the people for the authoritarian state.

It's just death

A durational installation positioning the viewer between opposing walls of AI-generated portraits—Ukrainian and Russian. As faces vanish into black voids, the work reduces mass tragedy to a routine data stream.

Darling

A hand-woven carpet crafted from black body bag plastic, mimicking a field of sedge grass. Through the ritualistic braiding of this synthetic soil, the installation transforms the floor into a texturized mass grave.

Boundaries of perception collection

This collection gathers concepts that span different periods of my life. Some originated long before the war, while others emerged in its shadow. What unites them is a shift away from direct documentation toward a more complex, multilayered inquiry.

The Divide

A minimalist diptych confronting Islamic and European cultural perceptions through opposing canvases. By juxtaposing a black veil of observation against a beige field of blindness, the work uses texture and geometry to materialize invisible barriers.

Glass ceiling

An installation featuring a low-hanging glass ceiling that forces viewers to stoop, physically enacting the constraints of gender inequality. The breakthroughs in the glass are edged with red crystal beads.

Digitalprint

An installation envisioning the future fossils of our technogenic era. Inside a black industrial cube, lampwork glass creatures glowing like 3D wireframes float in cold light.

Survival Game

A chess installation juxtaposing a massive transparent army of Civilization against a smaller opaque force of Nature. While visually dominant, the human side is strategically hopeless.

Form of existence

A interactive sculpture of a heart pierced by steel rods, which serve as the armature for a new personality. Viewers (or the artist) dress these needles in seven interchangeable skins.

The Garden

An optical sanctuary composed of mirrored vases resting on colorful saucers. Through reflection, the vessels absorb the hidden patterns beneath them, manifesting them as shimmering, illusory flowers.

Between the end and infinity

An infinity mirror tunnel visualizing a child's solution to an existential paradox. Confronting the terror of a boundless universe, the work populates the void with Pink Elephants.
