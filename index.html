Artist Statement

My artistic practice is an investigation into the fragility of systems, whether they are political regimes, psychological defenses, or ecological balances. I dissect how structure turns into ruin, and how that ruin is ritualized into a new, distorted reality.

As global tragedy dismantled the logic of everyday life, my art ceased to be a private conceptual exercise and became a necessity. Through the ritualistic repair of broken forms or the weaving of synthetic materials associated with death, I explore how individual lives are crushed by the machinery of history.

Ultimately, my work oscillates between the geopolitical and the deeply personal, revealing that the boundaries we accept are as transparent and fragile as the glass I shape.

Echo collection

This collection marks the moment my art ceased to be a private practice and became a necessity. Before the war, concepts lived in the drawer of my mind. But as reality fractured, the urge to materialize these thoughts became overwhelming.

Echoes gathers the works that were forced into existence by global tragedy, acting as a language when words are powerless. These pieces document the external collapse of logic and humanity, serving as tangible evidence of a world that has lost its structure.

Boundaries of perception collection

This collection gathers concepts that span different periods of my life. Some originated long before the war, while others emerged in its shadow. What unites them is a shift away from direct documentation toward a more complex, multilayered inquiry.

In Boundaries of Perception, the narrative evolves from the immediate to the existential. I move beyond the social surface to explore the deep, invisible architecture of the human condition. Here, individual trauma intersects with collective blindness (Survival Game), and personal myths (Pink Elephants) become the only way to navigate a fractured reality. These works invite the viewer into a space where the boundary between the external world and the internal self dissolves.
