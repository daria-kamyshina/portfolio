Crime or Punishment

kamysh #7 Art object

Frontal View / Relief Scale

The work takes the Russian Federation passport and enlarges it to architectural scale, transforming an object that fits in the pocket into a surface that confronts the body.

The relief is built from vertical prisms. Facing it directly, you see the passport cover: the emblem, the gold lettering, the red field. This is the image that functions as shorthand for citizenship, for suspicion, for the automatic sorting of people at borders.

Moving to either side dissolves the cover into ridges. The lateral faces carry text: on one side, documented transactions between Western institutions and the Russian elite; on the other, documented cases of persecution for resistance within Russia.

The scale refuses distance. The passport cannot be slipped away, cannot be hidden. It becomes a wall that occupies space and demands navigation.

The title asks a question that presumes choice: crime or punishment? The structure of the work suggests the premise itself is flawed. The document is neither. It is a given, a starting point.

Structural Schematic / Angles

Object & Process

Specifications

The author reserves the right to modify the specific documented transactions and persecution cases appearing on the lateral prism faces, as well as the precise dimensional proportions and material construction, provided the concept of "Architectural-Scale Passport as Unavoidable Surface" and the "Prismatic Revelation Through Viewer Movement" is preserved. The exact factual content and historical references may vary, maintaining the "Western Economic Complicity vs. Internal Resistance" dichotomy and the interrogation of the false "Crime or Punishment" premise.

Structure & Material

High-relief wall piece constructed from vertical triangular prisms on rigid support. Rigid paper or cardboard construction. Dimensions: 88 x 125 x 12 cm.

Optical Structure

Frontal view presents unified passport cover image; lateral views reveal text on prism faces. The reading requires movement, adjustment, the physical work of reconstructing a picture from fragments.

Data Content

Left-facing facets carry documented cases of Western economic complicity. Right-facing facets carry documented cases of internal resistance and state repression. Each text block includes specific factual identifiers (names, dates, legal references).
