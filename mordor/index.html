Mordor

kamysh #8 Art object

Concept sketch / atmosphere

A landscape flattened into a myth of absolute evil. Seen from the outside, it is a territory defined only by aggression and obscurity.

A room, floor to ceiling, is covered with grey paper. The space appears grim, closed, withdrawn. Under each sheet: a day, a fact, a name, an event from recent history the country. As visitors lift the grey layers, the concealed content emerges: images, text, the full tonal range of documentary color. The room shifts from monochrome severity to the specific hues of lived experience: dawn light, natural landscape, the varied palette of ordinary life.

To enter, you walk on it. The floor sheets tear and crumple underfoot, some events are lost to passage to time. On the walls, you choose: lift a sheet or leave it. Read or don't. Take it with you or drop it. The ceiling remains out of reach.

The space itself transforms from a dark, withdrawn mass into a breathing environment of pastel tones. The rejection and unease felt by early visitors gradually shift through the traces of all who passed: crumpled sheets on the floor, exposed layers on the walls, scattered remnants of others' choices.

Storyboard / Sequence

Object & Process

Specifications

The installation implies a variable scale depending on the exhibition space.

Format

Walk-through room installation. All interior surfaces — floor, walls, ceiling — covered with double-layer paper units: grey top sheet concealing printed content sheet below.

Content

Chronological documentation of recent history, drawn from daily news reports and documentary sources. Text in English. Visual materials include color photography.

Interaction

Floor sheets degrade through visitor traffic. Wall sheets can be manually lifted, removed, retained or discarded by visitors. Ceiling sheets remain physically inaccessible. Progressive revelation of concealed content transforms the chromatic and atmospheric quality of the space over the duration of the performance.

Materials

Grey paper, printed office paper, adhesive. Dimensions variable to venue.
