The Divide

kamysh #5 Art object

Concept sketch / installation plan

Two ways of looking that cannot be reconciled. One culture conceals its essence but observes the world intently. The other exposes itself completely but remains blind to what lies outside its own perception.

The work manifests this through two modes of concealment and revelation. A narrow aperture that sees out without being seen. A bar that covers the eyes of the exposed. Both at eye level, both at the same scale, both preventing the gaze from meeting.

The scarlet edges declare the wound of this separation. Most visible when the two are formally united: back to back, a single object with faces turned away from each other. At Face to face position, with viewers caught between, each looking at what the other cannot see.

No configuration resolves the gap. Two cultural architectures of visibility, equally absolute, equally incompatible.

Object & Process

Specifications

The artist reserves right to adjust palette, dimensions, and specific tonal values to exhibition conditions while maintaining the core compositional principle of the obscured gaze.

Materiality

Acrylic and spray paint on canvas. Two panels, 200 × 100 cm each. Edges painted scarlet.

Base & Intervention

Base layer: Monochrome grounds (black & beige-yellow) with multi-directional brushwork. Intervention: Horizontal aerosol bands at eye level (beige slit vs black bar).

Installation Logic

Confrontation (face to face), unity (back to back, double-sided), or wall-mounted (sequential). The configuration resolves the relationship between the two cultural architectures.
