It's just death

kamysh #10 Art object

Concept sketch / Gallery layout

The title carries the flatness of routine: "just" death, as if it were merely another item on a list. The work removes all theatrical horror — no blood, no noise, no narrative — to expose what remains when violence becomes administrative.

Two opposing walls of the gallery are transformed into total screens, placing the viewer in the gap between two identical machines. One side displays the fallen and missing from Ukraine; the other, from Russia. A dense grid of portraits covers each surface: synthetic identities generated by neural networks based on real obituaries.

The room is filled with the quiet, analog hum of time. The disappearance of each man functions as a binary event stripped of drama. Death is a dry mechanical click and an instantaneous hard cut to black. Missing in action is a dissolve into grey static and a burst of radio noise.

The cycle runs continuously for 7–10 days, calibrated to match the scale of actual losses. No viewer can witness it entirely. The work escapes completion, just as the counting never stops. Spatial sound ensures you are always too late: a click behind you, a turn, a black void where a face was.

Object & Process

Specifications

Material Disclaimer: Software and datasets may be updated. Core elements — synthetic grid, mechanical click/cut, opposing walls, asynchronous operation — remain fixed.

Format

Durational generative video installation. 2-channel projection, spatial sound, custom software. Variable dimensions. Cycle duration: 7–10 days.

System Logic

Custom software executes two elimination events in chaotic, asynchronous order.
Killed in action: Instant hard cut to black (Audio: dry mechanical click).
Missing in action: Rapid dissolve to grey static (Audio: burst of white noise).

Visuals

Two facing walls function as floor-to-ceiling projection surfaces. Each displays a dense, dynamically updating grid of approximately 50 portraits simultaneously. Portraits are synthetic identities based on anthropometric data and obituaries.
