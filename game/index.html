Survival Game

kamysh #2 Art object

Concept sketch / hand drawing

Survival Game exposes the arrogance of human pity towards nature. On the glass board, the massive transparent army of Civilization appears to aggressively crowd the smaller, opaque figures of Nature.

To an uninitiated viewer, this looks like environmental dominance: humanity crushing the ecosystem. But to a chess player, the board reveals the opposite reality. The transparent skyscrapers are in a hopeless position; their dominance is actually a trap.

The work mocks our anthropocentric guilt: we worry about saving the planet, forgetting that the planet is the Grandmaster, and we are the ones in Zugzwang.

Optical glass cast

Zugzwang position

Object & Process

Specifications

The author reserves the right to modify the specific chess notation of the position provided the concept of "Double Zugzwang favoring the minority side" is preserved.

Board & Dimensions

Glass, sandblasted grid. Board approximately 100 × 100 × 38 cm, total installation height approximately 50 cm.

Nature (White)

Matte/frosted glass, organic sculptures referencing flora and fauna. Lost wax casting. Height approximately 5–12 cm per piece.

Civilization (Black)

Transparent optical glass, solid rectangular blocks containing hollow internal cavities — negative impressions of skyscrapers. Kiln casting, coldworking. H: 8–15 cm.

Position

Fixed double zugzwang favoring the minority side (Nature). The dominance of the tall transparent pieces is structurally useless in this position.
