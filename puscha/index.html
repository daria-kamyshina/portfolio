Puscha

kamysh #8 Art object

Concept map / visual study

A landscape, neither city nor village, impossible to place. Grey-greens, dusty ochres, a sky in suspended tension. This is territory before it becomes a country, life before it is gathered into a name.

Over this surface, the word RUSSIA is laid out in medical bandages. A temporary fix, an attempt to suture what remains, to hold the fragments together. The bandages are white, barely distinct from the sky. They would peel away without pressure.

But the sutures are red. They pierce through the bandages, thick and crude, transforming letter by letter. R becomes P, S becomes C, I becomes H... RUSSIA becomes PUSCHA. The red does bleed. It carries the weight of Soviet red, of history that does not pass quietly.

The word that emerges is the dense, impenetrable forest. It is the regime that has consumed the name and rewritten it. The territory beneath remains ordinary, unchanged. The transformation happens in the violence of naming, in the confusion of state and land.

Complete work / 160 × 240 cm

Landscape detail / Collage

Object & Process

Specifications

The work relies on the tension between the smooth, illusory surface of the painting and the rough, physical texture of the textile.

Materiality

Oil on canvas, medical gauze, red thread. 160 × 240 cm.

Base Layer

Archetypal Central Russian landscape, geographically unidentifiable, rendered in muted, desaturated tones. The atmosphere holds meteorological uncertainty.

Intervention

The word RUSSIA constructed from medical bandages attached to the canvas surface. Red thread embroidery pierces and overlays the bandages, graphically altering the text to read PUSCHA.
