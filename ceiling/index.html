Glass ceiling

kamysh #6 Art object

Concept sketch / digital drawing

A transparent plane bisects the room at 180–185 cm. The surface is punctured by irregular openings. These are the breakthroughs, the success stories cited as proof that the ceiling no longer exists. Yet the structure remains intact. The edges of each opening are jagged, sharp, encrusted with red crystals that catch the light. They glitter like achievement. They look like blood.

For most adults, passage requires stooping, bending, a posture of submission that registers in the body before it reaches language. Through the openings, one can straighten, but the space itself offers no relief. The wound is glamorized.

The work addresses inequality sustained by survivorship bias: individual ascents held up as evidence of open access.

Object & Process

Specifications

Alternative transparent materials or red crystalline media are permitted provided the visual effect of bleeding wounds and the interactive concept of restrictive height versus passage remain intact.

Materiality

Tempered glass, red crystal beads, steel suspension system. Height: 180–185 cm. Variable room dimensions.

Installation

The total area of glass coverage and placement of openings may be adapted to venue architecture.

Structure

Horizontal transparent plane suspended across the exhibition space. The height is fixed and protected; it enforces physical restriction as a core component of the work.

Breaches

Large irregular openings in the glass, minimum diameter approximately 50 cm. The perimeters are non-geometric, sharp, densely encrusted with red crystals. Vertical threads suspend additional crystals, extending the form downward.
