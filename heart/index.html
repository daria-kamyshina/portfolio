Form of existence

kamysh #12 Art object

Concept sketch / hand drawing

Form of Existence explores how trauma reshapes the architecture of the soul. Needles that once pierced the heart remain within, becoming its supporting framework—a skeleton upon which a new, hypersensitive personality grows.

The installation is a 1.5-meter interactive sculpture: an anatomical heart pierced by steel rods. It functions as a transformer with interchangeable "skins," each representing a psychological defense mechanism. Viewers can dress the naked needles with materials ranging from Concrete (Barriers) and Mirrors (Reflectors) to Feathers (Grandeur) and Felt (Softeners).

This process can be a durational ritual (changing daily) or a participatory act where the audience collectively clothes the pain. By interacting with the sharp steel, the viewer transforms a weapon into a shield, finding a unique, bizarre harmony on the soil of pain.

Object & Process

Specifications

The author reserves the right to expand the library of skins and adjust the number of rods, maintaining the core principle of radiating steel rods forming a heart outline.

Core & Armature

Hyper-realistic anatomical heart (~30cm) with industrial rigidity. Removable steel rods up to 100cm screw into the core, forming a spiked exoskeleton. Diameter 150 cm.

Interaction

Viewers attach modules to sharp rod tips, transitioning the object from danger to safety.

Modular Skins

7 interchangeable sets attaching to rod tips:
- Reflectors (Mirror squares)
- Ornaments (Chrome spheres)
- Facades (Tyvek floral)
- Grandeur (Ostrich feathers)
- Softeners (Felt spheres)
- Ghosts (Tulle)
- Barriers (Concrete cubes)
